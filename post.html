5 signs your combi oven needs a service

PTPT&L Service Team 12 June 2026 4 min read

A combi oven rarely fails without warning. Long before it stops mid-service, it drops hints — uneven bakes, creeping pre-heat times, the odd error code you reset and forget. Catching these early is the difference between a quick service call and a kitchen at a standstill on a Friday night.

Here are the five signs our technicians see most often, and what each one is usually telling you.

1. Cooking is uneven or inconsistent

If trays that used to come out identically are now browning on one side, or you're rotating product more than you used to, the oven is no longer holding an even temperature. Worn door seals, a failing fan motor or a drifting temperature probe are the usual culprits — all of which get worse, and more expensive, the longer they're left.

2. Error codes keep appearing

An occasional code that clears on a restart is easy to ignore. But repeat codes are the oven's own diagnostics flagging a fault — often with the water system, steam generator or temperature sensors. Note the exact code before you reset it; it tells a technician where to look and usually shortens the repair.

3. Steam or water is leaking

Water pooling under the unit or steam escaping around the door points to perished seals, a blocked drain or a scaled-up steam generator. Beyond the slip hazard, leaks let heat and humidity escape, so the oven works harder, costs more to run and cooks less predictably.

4. Pre-heat takes longer than it used to

Sluggish pre-heat and weak heat recovery after the door opens are classic signs of scale build-up, a tired heating element or a fan that's slowing down. Quietly, this also pushes up your energy bill — the oven is drawing power for longer to reach the same set point.

5. New noises or smells

Grinding or rattling usually means a fan bearing on the way out; a burning or electrical smell should stop the oven immediately and bring a technician in. Neither is something to cook through — both tend to escalate from a part replacement into a major repair.

"A serviced combi oven that's caught early is a callout. Left alone, the same fault becomes a breakdown — and a breakdown becomes lost covers."

The bottom line

Most combi oven failures are gradual, which means they're preventable. A scheduled service catches worn seals, scale and tired components before they take the oven offline — and keeps it running efficiently in between. If you've noticed any of the signs above, it's worth booking a look-over now rather than waiting for the oven to make the decision for you.
